Booking Platform Integration

Connect your booking platform to IntroLinq to earn a share of every session booked through our publisher network.

How it works

IntroLinq embeds a widget on publisher blogs that surfaces relevant experts as readers scroll. When a reader clicks "Book a call", they pass through IntroLinq and arrive on your booking page with attribution parameters in the URL. When the booking completes, you send a webhook back to us.

Reader clicks "Book a call"

IntroLinq logs the click and redirects to your expert's booking page with click_id and aid appended to the URL.

Store the attribution params

On page load, read click_id and aid from the URL and store them in a cookie (30-day expiry recommended). This ensures attribution survives if the reader books later.

Booking completes - send a webhook

When a booking is confirmed, POST to the IntroLinq webhook endpoint with the booking details and the stored attribution params.

Attribution URL params

When IntroLinq redirects a reader to your platform, the following params are appended to the expert's booking URL:

ParameterDescription
click_idUnique UUID for this click - the primary attribution key. Store this and return it in the webhook.
aidPublisher slug - identifies which publisher's site the reader came from.
refAlways introlinq - lets you identify IntroLinq traffic.
campaignThe article URL the reader was on when they clicked.
langReader's browser language (e.g. en-GB, fr).
Example URL your booking page will receive:
https://yourbookingplatform.com/expert/jane-smith?click_id=4f3a…&aid=entrepreneurs-collective&ref=introlinq&campaign=https%3A%2F%2F…

Webhook endpoint

Request

POST https://www.introlinq.com/api/dashboard?action=booking&pub={your-platform-slug}
Content-Type: application/json
x-introlinq-secret: {your_secret_key}

The pub parameter is your fixed platform identifier (e.g. open-intro) — it stays the same for every booking you send. IntroLinq resolves which publisher to pay from the click_id.

Payload

FieldTypeDescription
click_idstring requiredThe click_id received in the attribution URL. This is how IntroLinq identifies which publisher referred the reader and calculates their payout.
booking_amountnumber requiredThe amount IntroLinq uses to calculate the publisher payout — as agreed in your partner contract. The publisher receives their revenue share percentage of this figure.
currencystring requiredISO 4217 currency code, e.g. GBP, EUR, USD.
expert_idinteger optionalYour internal expert ID.
expert_namestring optionalExpert's full name - shown in the publisher's commission email.
testboolean optionalSet true during integration testing. No booking is recorded, no email sent - but Slack fires so you can verify delivery. Default: false.

Example

{
  "click_id": "4f3a9b2c-1234-4d56-a789-0abc12def345",
  "expert_id": 38,
  "expert_name": "Jane Smith",
  "booking_amount": 15,
  "currency": "GBP"
}

Response

200 OK

{
  "ok": true,
  "publisher_payout": 10.50,
  "publisher": "entrepreneurs-collective",
  "expert": "Jane Smith"
}

Errors

401 - Invalid or missing secret
400 - Missing booking_amount
400 - Cannot resolve publisher
404 - Publisher not found

Testing your integration

Before going live, verify your integration using test mode. Set "test": true in the payload - IntroLinq will validate the request, resolve the publisher, calculate the payout, and fire a Slack notification, but will not record the booking or email the publisher.

curl -X POST "https://www.introlinq.com/api/dashboard?action=booking&pub=open-intro" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -H "x-introlinq-secret: YOUR_SECRET" \
  -d '{
    "click_id": "test-click-id-123",
    "expert_name": "Test Expert",
    "booking_amount": 15,
    "currency": "GBP",
    "test": true
  }'
Expected response: {"ok":true,"test":true,"publisher_payout":10.5,"publisher":"entrepreneurs-collective","expert":"Test Expert"}

Authentication

All webhook requests must include your secret key in the x-introlinq-secret header. Your key is shared with you directly - never include it in client-side code or public repositories.

We recommend rotating your key every 6–12 months. Contact us to issue a new one.

Commission & payouts

Each publisher has a configured revenue share (default 70%). IntroLinq calculates and logs the publisher_payout automatically based on the booking_amount you send. The publisher receives an email notification for each booking, and a weekly summary of their earnings.

Payouts are issued separately - the webhook is for attribution and notification only.
