03 What Introlinq Is

One line of code. Every article becomes a doorway.

  1. 01

    Publisher installs a single script tag — no redesign, no editorial workflow change.

  2. 02

    Introlinq's matching engine reads the page and finds phrases that are genuinely relevant to a bookable expert — not keyword matches, contextual fit.

  3. 03

    Relevant phrases become interactive. Readers discover experts, mentors, or services that help them take the next step, without ever leaving the article.

Under the hood Matching runs on Claude, with prompt caching so repeat publishers cost a fraction of a first scan. A hard domain-fit rule stops the model bridging a generalist expert to a specialist topic just to force a match, and a scan cache means an article is never re-evaluated by the model twice.

Commission terms vary by partner — from Clarity's 15% platform fee to Fiverr's 25%+10% revshare or MentorCruise's 50% recurring. 4.5% is the standard, provable baseline; blended take rate improves as higher-commission partners join the supply mix.

Cost to serve Scanning and matching one article costs roughly £0.01–£0.08 in model inference (Claude Haiku 4.5, cache-optimised) — measured per scan, not estimated. Against £22.50 average net revenue per booking, the matching engine itself carries effectively no marginal cost. Gross margin is a supply-partner-negotiation problem, not a compute problem.
